Linguistic Divergence Score (LDS) — Methodology

The Linguistic Divergence Score quantifies structural divergence between knowledge graphs extracted from different languages:

LDS(A, B) = 1 − (J(V_a,V_b) + J(E_a,E_b))/2

A value of 0 indicates identical structure; near 1 indicates maximal divergence. A human validation study (N=30, 10 per language) is in preparation.

Structural Analysis Report

Automated analysis of the CognitiveSpace knowledge graph. Generated from 68 textbooks, 574 concepts, 3538 relations across ZH/EN/DE.

1. Corpus Composition

The knowledge graph is constructed from 68 textbooks across three languages (45 Chinese, 20 English, 10 German), spanning four education levels. The Chinese corpus provides the most extensive coverage of fundamental mathematics, while English and German corpora focus on secondary and tertiary education.

2. Education Level Distribution

University concepts constitute the largest share, with elementary concepts serving as foundational anchors with highest graph centrality. This confirms a "knowledge core → expansion" pattern.

3. Cross-Language Alignment

Concepts are mapped across all three languages, with ZH having the highest exclusive coverage from curricula lacking Western counterparts.

4. Structural Observations

The graph is nearly fully connected (only 2 isolated nodes, <0.5%). Elementary concepts serve as structural anchors. Concepts appearing in multiple languages exhibit structurally similar neighborhoods, suggesting language-independent mathematical invariants.

5. Linguistic Divergence Score (LDS) (Planned)

A human validation study (N=30) is in preparation to establish baseline LDS values for mathematics education across ZH/EN/DE.
