Knowledge and power are deeply intertwined. Francis Bacon's famous declaration "Knowledge is power" captures the transformative potential of understanding. In modern societies, knowledge is the primary driver of economic growth and social progress. The relationship between knowledge and power has been analyzed by thinkers from Plato to Foucault — knowledge shapes what we can do, and power determines what knowledge is produced and shared. In the information age, data has become a new form of knowledge, creating new power dynamics. The digital divide — between those with access to knowledge and those without — is a critical social issue. Education systems play a crucial role in distributing knowledge equitably. Critical thinking and information literacy are essential skills for navigating a world where knowledge is both abundant and contested.
