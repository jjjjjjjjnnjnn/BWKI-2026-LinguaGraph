Time is one of the most fundamental concepts in physics and philosophy. In physics, time is the fourth dimension — Einstein's relativity showed that time is not absolute but relative, affected by speed and gravity. In Western philosophy, time is often understood as linear: it flows from past through present to future, and cannot be reversed. This contrasts with cyclical conceptions found in many Eastern cultures, where time is seen as recurring patterns. Language shapes our experience of time: in English, we "spend" time, "waste" time, or "save" time — metaphors that treat time as a resource. The physics of time raises profound questions: Does time have a direction? Is the future predetermined? These questions connect quantum mechanics, cosmology, and human consciousness. Understanding time requires both scientific rigor and philosophical reflection.
