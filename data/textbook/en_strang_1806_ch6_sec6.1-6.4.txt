Chapter 6 Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

6.1 Introduction to Eigenvalues

Ax = λx — the eigenvalue equation.

Eigenvalues are roots of det(A - λI) = 0.
Eigenvectors are nonzero vectors in the null space of (A - λI).

6.2 Diagonalization

A is diagonalizable if A = SΛS⁻¹, where S contains eigenvectors and Λ has eigenvalues on the diagonal.

Condition: A must have n linearly independent eigenvectors.

6.3 Applications

1. Computing Aⁿ = SΛⁿS⁻¹
2. Solving differential equations dx/dt = Ax
3. Markov chains and Google's PageRank
4. Principal Component Analysis (PCA)

6.4 Symmetric Matrices

Real symmetric matrices have real eigenvalues and orthogonal eigenvectors.
Spectral Theorem: A = QΛQ^T where Q is orthogonal.

Key relationship: Eigenvalues capture the essential behavior of linear transformations — they reveal how a transformation scales along special directions.
