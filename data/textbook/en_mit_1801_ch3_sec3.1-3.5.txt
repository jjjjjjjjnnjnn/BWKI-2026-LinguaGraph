MIT 18.01SC Single Variable Calculus — Chapter 3: The Definite Integral

3.1 The Definite Integral

∫_a^b f(x)dx = lim_{n→∞} Σ_{i=1}^n f(x_i^*) Δx

Riemann sum interpretation: area under the curve.

3.2 The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Part 1: d/dx ∫_a^x f(t)dt = f(x)
Part 2: ∫_a^b f(x)dx = F(b) - F(a) where F' = f

This theorem connects differentiation and integration as inverse operations.

3.3 The Substitution Rule (u-substitution)

∫ f(g(x))g'(x)dx = ∫ f(u)du where u = g(x)

The chain rule in reverse.

3.4 Areas Between Curves

Area = ∫_a^b |f(x) - g(x)|dx

3.5 Volumes of Revolution

Disk method: V = π ∫_a^b [f(x)]^2 dx
Washer method: V = π ∫_a^b ([R(x)]^2 - [r(x)]^2) dx
Shell method: V = 2π ∫_a^b x·f(x) dx

The Fundamental Theorem is the crown jewel of calculus — it unifies the two seemingly different operations of differentiation and integration.
