Identity is the sense of who we are — our self-concept shaped by personal experiences, social relationships, and cultural context. In Western philosophy, identity has been explored from Descartes' "I think, therefore I am" to modern theories of social identity. Our identity is constructed through multiple layers: personal identity (individual traits and experiences), social identity (group memberships and roles), and cultural identity (shared values, traditions, and language). Language plays a crucial role in shaping identity — the words we use, the stories we tell, and the narratives we construct define how we understand ourselves. In a globalized world, identity is increasingly fluid and multifaceted. People navigate between multiple cultural identities, creating hybrid forms of self-expression. The tension between individual uniqueness and collective belonging is a central challenge of modern identity.
