Khan Academy 6th-8th Grade Mathematics

=== 6th Grade ===

6.RP: Ratios and Proportional Relationships
- Understand the concept of a ratio and use ratio language
- Understand the concept of a unit rate associated with a ratio
- Use ratio and rate reasoning to solve real-world problems
- Make tables of equivalent ratios and find missing values
- Solve unit rate problems including unit pricing and constant speed

6.NS: Number System
- Fluently divide multi-digit numbers using the standard algorithm
- Fluently add, subtract, multiply, and divide multi-digit decimals
- Find the greatest common factor of two whole numbers ≤ 100
- Find the least common multiple of two whole numbers ≤ 12
- Understand positive and negative numbers on a number line
- Understand ordering and absolute value of rational numbers

6.EE: Expressions and Equations
- Write and evaluate numerical expressions involving whole-number exponents
- Write, read, and evaluate expressions in which letters stand for numbers
- Apply the properties of operations to generate equivalent expressions
- Identify when two expressions are equivalent
- Understand solving an equation as a process of reasoning
- Write an inequality of the form x > c or x < c to represent a constraint

6.G: Geometry
- Find the area of right triangles, other triangles, special quadrilaterals, and polygons
- Find the volume of a right rectangular prism with fractional edge lengths
- Draw polygons in the coordinate plane given coordinates for the vertices
- Represent three-dimensional figures using nets made up of rectangles and triangles

6.SP: Statistics and Probability
- Recognize a statistical question as one that anticipates variability
- Summarize numerical data sets in relation to their context
- Give quantitative measures of center (median, mean) and variability (interquartile range, mean absolute deviation)
- Display numerical data on a number line, dot plot, histogram, and box plot

=== 7th Grade ===

7.RP: Proportional Relationships
- Compute unit rates associated with ratios of fractions
- Decide whether two quantities are in a proportional relationship
- Identify the constant of proportionality from tables, equations, and graphs
- Explain what a point (x, y) on the graph means in terms of the situation

7.NS: Number System
- Apply and extend previous understandings of addition and subtraction to add and subtract rational numbers
- Apply and extend previous understandings of multiplication and division to multiply and divide rational numbers
- Convert a rational number to a decimal using long division

7.EE: Expressions and Equations
- Use properties of operations to add, subtract, factor, and expand linear expressions with rational coefficients
- Rewrite an expression in different forms to shed light on the problem
- Solve multistep linear equations and inequalities with rational number coefficients
- Use variables to represent quantities and construct simple equations and inequalities

7.G: Geometry
- Solve problems involving scale drawings of geometric figures
- Draw geometric shapes with given conditions using rulers and protractors
- Describe the two-dimensional figures that result from slicing three-dimensional figures
- Know the formulas for area and circumference of a circle and use them to solve problems
- Know the facts about angles: complementary, supplementary, vertical, and adjacent angles
- Solve real-world and mathematical problems involving area, volume, and surface area

7.SP: Statistics and Probability
- Use random sampling to draw inferences about a population
- Draw informal comparative inferences about two populations
- Investigate chance processes and develop, use, and evaluate probability models

=== 8th Grade ===

8.NS: Number System
- Know that numbers that are not rational are called irrational
- Approximate irrational numbers by rational numbers
- Use rational approximations of irrational numbers to compare and locate on a number line

8.EE: Expressions and Equations
- Work with radicals and integer exponents
- Know and apply the properties of integer exponents
- Use square root and cube root symbols to represent solutions
- Graph proportional relationships and interpret the unit rate as the slope
- Use similar triangles to explain why the slope m is the same between any two distinct points

8.F: Functions
- Understand that a function assigns to each input exactly one output
- Compare properties of two functions each represented differently
- Interpret the equation y = mx + b as defining a linear function
- Construct a function to model a linear relationship between two quantities
- Describe qualitatively the functional relationship between two quantities by analyzing a graph

8.G: Geometry
- Verify experimentally the properties of rotations, reflections, and translations
- Understand that a two-dimensional figure is congruent to another if obtained from the first by rotations, reflections, translations
- Describe the effect of dilations, translations, rotations, and reflections on figures
- Understand and use the Pythagorean Theorem
- Solve real-world and mathematical problems involving volume of cylinders, cones, and spheres

8.SP: Statistics and Probability
- Construct and interpret scatter plots for bivariate measurement data
- Describe patterns such as clustering, outliers, positive/negative association
- Use the equation of a linear model to solve problems
- Construct and interpret a two-way table of data

Core chain: ratios → rational numbers → expressions → functions → Pythagorean theorem → statistics
