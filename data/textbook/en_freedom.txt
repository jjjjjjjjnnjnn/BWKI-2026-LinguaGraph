Freedom is a fundamental right in democratic societies. It encompasses the liberty to make choices, express opinions, and live according to one's own values, within the bounds of law. The concept of freedom has evolved through centuries of philosophical and political thought — from John Locke's natural rights to John Stuart Mill's harm principle. Freedom includes civil liberties such as freedom of speech, religion, and assembly. However, freedom is not absolute: it must be balanced with responsibility and respect for others' rights. The tension between individual freedom and social order is a central question in political philosophy. In practice, freedom requires both legal protections and a culture that values individual autonomy while recognizing collective obligations.
