Chapter 4 L'Hôpital's Rule and Series

4.1 L'Hôpital's Rule

The 0/0 Case: If lim f(x) = lim g(x) = 0, and lim f'(x)/g'(x) exists, then
lim f(x)/g(x) = lim f'(x)/g'(x)

The ∞/∞ Case: If lim f(x) = lim g(x) = ∞, and lim f'(x)/g'(x) exists, then
lim f(x)/g(x) = lim f'(x)/g'(x)

Other Indeterminate Forms:
- 0·∞: Rewrite as 0/(1/∞) or ∞/(1/0)
- ∞-∞: Combine fractions
- 1^∞, 0^0, ∞^0: Take logarithm, then apply L'Hôpital

Examples:
1. lim_{x→0} sin x / x = lim_{x→0} cos x / 1 = 1
2. lim_{x→∞} (ln x) / x = lim_{x→∞} (1/x) / 1 = 0
3. lim_{x→0} (e^x - 1 - x) / x² = lim_{x→0} (e^x - 1) / 2x = lim_{x→0} e^x / 2 = 1/2

L'Hôpital's Rule connects differentiation to limit computation. It requires the existence of the limit of the ratio of derivatives, which is a stronger condition than the existence of the original limit.

4.2 Taylor Series

Taylor's Theorem: If f has n+1 derivatives near a, then
f(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x-a) + f''(a)(x-a)²/2! + ... + R_n

Maclaurin Series (a = 0):
- e^x = Σ xⁿ/n! for all x
- sin x = Σ (-1)ⁿx²ⁿ⁺¹/(2n+1)! for all x
- cos x = Σ (-1)ⁿx²ⁿ/(2n)! for all x
- 1/(1-x) = Σ xⁿ for |x| < 1
- ln(1+x) = Σ (-1)ⁿ⁻¹xⁿ/n for -1 < x ≤ 1

Taylor series generalizes the tangent line approximation to use all higher-order derivatives. The remainder term R_n determines the accuracy of the polynomial approximation.

Key relationship: Taylor series connects differentiation, integration, and infinite series — three major topics in calculus unified through the concept of derivatives.
