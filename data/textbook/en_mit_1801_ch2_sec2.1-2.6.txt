MIT 18.01SC Single Variable Calculus — Chapter 2: Applications of Differentiation

2.1 Related Rates

If two quantities are related and both change with time, relate their rates of change.

Example: A ladder slides down a wall. If the bottom moves away at 2 ft/s, how fast does the top slide down when the bottom is 6 ft from the wall?

2.2 Linear Approximation

f(x) ≈ f(a) + f'(a)(x - a) near x = a.

The tangent line provides the best linear approximation.

2.3 Curve Sketching

Using f', f'' to determine:
- Increasing/decreasing intervals (f' > 0 or < 0)
- Concavity (f'' > 0 or < 0)
- Local max/min (first or second derivative test)
- Inflection points (f'' changes sign)

2.4 Optimization

Find maximum or minimum of f on [a, b]:
1. Find critical points: f'(x) = 0 or f' undefined
2. Evaluate f at critical points and endpoints
3. Compare values

2.5 L'Hospital's Rule

For 0/0 or ∞/∞ forms: lim f(x)/g(x) = lim f'(x)/g'(x)

2.6 Mean Value Theorem

If f is continuous on [a,b] and differentiable on (a,b), then ∃c ∈ (a,b) such that f'(c) = [f(b)-f(a)]/(b-a).

Applications prove that if f' = 0 on an interval, then f is constant.
