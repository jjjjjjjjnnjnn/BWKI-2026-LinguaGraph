The relationship between individual and society is one of the central questions in sociology, philosophy, and political science. From Aristotle's observation that humans are "social animals" to modern theories of social contract, thinkers have explored how individuals and communities shape each other. Society provides the framework within which individuals develop — through education, shared norms, and collective institutions. In return, individuals contribute to society through their labor, creativity, and participation. The tension between individual freedom and social responsibility is a defining challenge of modern democracies. Key concepts include: social contract theory (Hobbes, Locke, Rousseau), functionalism (Durkheim), and conflict theory (Marx). Understanding this relationship requires recognizing that neither the individual nor society exists in isolation — they are mutually constitutive.
