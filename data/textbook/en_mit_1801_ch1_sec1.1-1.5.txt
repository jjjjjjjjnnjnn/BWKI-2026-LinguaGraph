MIT 18.01SC Single Variable Calculus — Chapter 1: Differentiation

1.1 Definition of the Derivative

The derivative of f at a is: f'(a) = lim_{h→0} [f(a+h) - f(a)] / h

Geometric meaning: slope of tangent line at point (a, f(a)).

Physical meaning: instantaneous rate of change.

1.2 Basic Differentiation Rules

Power Rule: d/dx(x^n) = nx^{n-1}
Trigonometric: d/dx(sin x) = cos x, d/dx(cos x) = -sin x
Exponential: d/dx(e^x) = e^x

Product Rule: d/dx(uv) = u'v + uv'
Quotient Rule: d/dx(u/v) = (u'v - uv')/v^2
Chain Rule: d/dx[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) · g'(x)

1.3 Limits and Continuity

A function f is continuous at a if lim_{x→a} f(x) = f(a).

Key limit: lim_{x→0} sin(x)/x = 1

Squeeze Theorem: if g(x) ≤ f(x) ≤ h(x) and lim g = lim h = L, then lim f = L.

1.4 Implicit Differentiation

Differentiate both sides of an equation with respect to x, treating y as a function of x.

Example: d/dx(x^2 + y^2) = d/dx(1) → 2x + 2y·y' = 0 → y' = -x/y

1.5 Inverse Functions and Their Derivatives

If y = f(x) and x = g(y) are inverses, then g'(y) = 1/f'(g(y)).

(d/dx)ln x = 1/x, (d/dx)a^x = a^x ln a

Calculus is the mathematics of change — differentiation measures instantaneous change, integration accumulates change.
