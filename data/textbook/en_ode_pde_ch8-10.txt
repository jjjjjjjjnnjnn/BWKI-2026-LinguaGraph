Chapter 8 Second-Order Linear ODEs

8.1 Homogeneous Equations with Constant Coefficients

ay'' + by' + cy = 0
Characteristic equation: ar² + br + c = 0
Three cases: two distinct real roots, repeated root, complex conjugate roots

8.2 Nonhomogeneous Equations

Method of Undetermined Coefficients: guess y* based on form of f(x)
Variation of Parameters: y* = u₁y₁ + u₂y₂ where u₁', u₂' solve a system

8.3 Applications

Spring-mass: mx'' + cx' + kx = F(t)
- Undamped: simple harmonic motion
- Damped: exponential decay
- Forced: resonance when driving frequency ≈ natural frequency

RLC circuits: LQ'' + RQ' + Q/C = E(t) (analogous to spring-mass)

Chapter 9 Systems of ODEs

9.1 First-Order Systems

x' = Ax where A is the coefficient matrix
Solution: x = e^(At)x₀

9.2 Eigenvalue Method

If A has eigenvalues λ and eigenvectors v:
General solution: x = c₁e^(λ₁t)v₁ + c₂e^(λ₂t)v₂

Key relationship: Eigenvalues of the coefficient matrix determine the qualitative behavior — stable nodes, spirals, saddles, centers.

Chapter 10 Partial Differential Equations

10.1 The Three Classical PDEs

Wave equation: u_tt = c²u_xx (vibration, sound, light)
Heat equation: u_t = k u_xx (diffusion, temperature)
Laplace equation: u_xx + u_yy = 0 (steady-state, potentials)

10.2 Separation of Variables

Assume u(x,t) = X(x)T(t), substitute into PDE, separate into ODEs.

10.3 Fourier Series

f(x) = a₀/2 + Σ[aₙcos(nπx/L) + bₙsin(nπx/L)]

The eigenfunctions from separation of variables form a complete orthogonal system — this is why Fourier series appear naturally in PDE solutions.

Key relationship: PDEs extend ODEs to functions of multiple variables. Fourier analysis provides the bridge between separation of variables and series solutions.
