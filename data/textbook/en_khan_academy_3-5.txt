Khan Academy 3rd-5th Grade Mathematics

=== 3rd Grade ===

3.OA: Operations and Algebraic Thinking
- Understand multiplication as equal groups
- Understand division as sharing equally
- Use multiplication and division within 100
- Solve two-step word problems using addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division
- Identify arithmetic patterns (e.g., multiples of 5)

3.NBT: Number and Operations in Base Ten
- Use place value to round whole numbers to the nearest 10 or 100
- Fluently add within 1000 using place value understanding
- Multiply one-digit whole numbers by multiples of 10 (e.g., 9 × 80)

3.NF: Number and Operations — Fractions
- Understand a fraction 1/b as one part of a whole partitioned into b equal parts
- Understand a fraction a/b as a parts of size 1/b
- Represent fractions on a number line
- Explain equivalence of fractions (1/2 = 2/4)
- Compare fractions with same numerator or denominator

3.MD: Measurement and Data
- Tell and write time to the nearest minute
- Measure and estimate liquid volumes and masses
- Draw scaled picture graphs and bar graphs
- Solve one-step addition and subtraction word problems using data
- Relate area to multiplication and addition
- Recognize area as additive

3.G: Geometry
- Understand that shapes from different categories may share attributes
- Partition shapes into parts with equal areas, express area as unit fractions

=== 4th Grade ===

4.OA: Operations and Algebraic Thinking
- Interpret a multiplication equation as a comparison (35 = 5 × 7 as 35 is 5 times as many as 7)
- Solve multistep word problems with whole numbers
- Find all factor pairs for a whole number in the range 1-100
- Determine whether a whole number is a multiple of a given one-digit number

4.NBT: Number and Operations in Base Ten
- Generalize place value understanding for multi-digit whole numbers
- Read and write multi-digit whole numbers using base-ten numerals, number names, and expanded form
- Compare two multi-digit numbers based on digit meanings
- Fluently add and subtract multi-digit whole numbers using the standard algorithm
- Multiply a whole number of up to four digits by a one-digit number
- Divide up to four-digit dividends by one-digit divisors

4.NF: Number and Operations — Fractions
- Explain why a fraction a/b is equivalent to (a×n)/(b×n)
- Compare two fractions with different numerators and denominators
- Understand decimal notation for fractions and compare decimal fractions
- Add fractions with like denominators
- Multiply a fraction by a whole number
- Solve word problems involving multiplication of a fraction by a whole number

4.MD: Measurement and Data
- Know relative sizes of measurement units within one system
- Use the four operations to solve word problems involving measurement
- Apply area and perimeter formulas for rectangles
- Make a line plot to display data measured in fractions

4.G: Geometry
- Draw points, lines, line segments, rays, angles (right, acute, obtuse), and perpendicular and parallel lines
- Classify two-dimensional figures based on parallel or perpendicular lines
- Identify lines of symmetry

=== 5th Grade ===

5.NBT: Number and Operations in Base Ten
- Understand the place value system (each place is 10 times the place to its right)
- Read, write, and compare decimals to thousandths
- Use place value understanding to round decimals to any place
- Fluently multiply multi-digit whole numbers using the standard algorithm
- Find whole-number quotients with up to four-digit dividends and two-digit divisors
- Add, subtract, multiply, and divide decimals to hundredths

5.NF: Number and Operations — Fractions
- Add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators
- Interpret a fraction as division of numerator by denominator
- Multiply a fraction or whole number by a fraction
- Find the area of a rectangle with fractional side lengths
- Divide unit fractions by whole numbers and whole numbers by unit fractions

5.MD: Measurement and Data
- Convert among different-sized standard measurement units
- Make a line plot to display data measured in fractions
- Recognize volume as an attribute of three-dimensional figures
- Find volumes of right rectangular prisms with whole-number edge lengths

5.G: Geometry
- Graph points on the coordinate plane to solve real-world and mathematical problems
- Classify two-dimensional figures into categories based on properties

Core chain: place value → multi-digit operations → fractions → decimals → measurement → geometry
