Freedom is the power or right to speak, act, and change as one wants without hindrance or restraint. It connects to both liberty and autonomy, described as giving oneself one's own laws. In philosophy and religion, freedom is often linked to free will, contrasted with determinism or predestination.

Political freedom relates to liberty and autonomy, with distinctions between free societies and dictatorships. Civil rights discussions contrast freedom with slavery, with ongoing debates about whether freedom belongs to all groups equally. Key freedoms include assembly, association, choice, and speech.

John Stuart Mill differentiated freedom (ability to do as one wills) from liberty (absence of arbitrary restraints accounting for others' rights). Isaiah Berlin's famous distinction between positive and negative freedom is central: negative liberty means freedom from limiting forces such as fear, want, or discrimination; positive liberty is the ability to fulfill one's purposes.

Charles Taylor elaborated that negative liberty is the ability to do what one wants without external obstacles, while positive liberty is the ability to fulfill one's purposes. Wendy Hui Kyong Chun offers an institutional distinction: liberty is linked to human subjectivity and institutions, while freedom is not. Orlando Patterson argued that freedom arose from the yearnings of slaves.

Philip Pettit, Quentin Skinner, and John P. McCormick identified freedom from domination as a defining aspect of freedom. A distinction is drawn between operative control (directing one's daily actions) and reserve control (freedom depending on another's whim). Both are necessary for democracy and freedom.

Free will is described as the idea that entities can willingly choose an arbitrary option when presented, particularly in human behavior. Determinism stands in opposition. The article notes this as a long-debated philosophical topic with scientific arguments on both sides. Related concepts include freedom of thought, freedom of speech, the harm principle, internet freedom, and freedom indices.
